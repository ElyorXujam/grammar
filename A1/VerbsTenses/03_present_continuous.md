# English Grammar Core: Present Continuous

### Quick Overview
The **Present Continuous** shifts focus entirely to **temporary situations, actions happening at this exact moment**, or **current trends**. It shows that an action is in progress and unfinished.

---

### Structural Breakdown
$$	ext{Subject} + 	ext{am/is/are} + 	ext{Verb-ing}$$

* **Positive:** *We **are upgrading** our production server as we speak.*
* **Negative:** *I **am not using** that software environment today; feel free to modify it.*
* **Question:** ***Is she conducting** the job interviews right now?*

---

### Present Simple vs. Present Continuous

| Aspect | Present Simple | Present Continuous |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Focus** | Permanent / Habitual | Temporary / Happening Now |
| **Example** | *I **work** from home.* (Permanent job status) | *I **am working** from home today.* (Just for today) |
| **Signal Words** | *Always, typically, every week.* | *Currently, right now, at the moment.* |

---

### Noticeable Feedback & Common Pitfalls
* ⚠️ **The Missing Link:** Never drop the verb "to be". 
    * *Incorrect:* `They working on the bug fix.` 
    * *Correct:* `They **are** working on the bug fix.`
* ⚠️ **State Verb Trap:** Do not use continuous forms with verbs of emotion or possession. 
    * *Incorrect:* `I am wanting to clarify this.`
    * *Correct:* `I **want** to clarify this.`
