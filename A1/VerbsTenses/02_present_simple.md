# English Grammar Core: Present Simple

### Quick Overview
We use the **Present Simple** to communicate **habits, daily routines, permanent situations**, and **universal facts**. It represents things that are generally true over a long period, not just at this exact second.

---

### Structural Breakdown

#### 1. Affirmative (The Third Person -s)
The only structural trap here is the third-person singular (**he, she, it**). You must add **-s** or **-es** to the verb.
* *I / You / We / They **manage** multiple software projects simultaneously.*
* *He / She / It **manages** multiple software projects simultaneously.*

#### 2. Negatives & Questions (The "Do/Does" Auxiliaries)
To form negatives and questions, you must introduce **do** or **does**. Once **does** is introduced, the main verb drops its **-s** modifier completely.
* *Negative:* `She doesn't work here anymore.` *(Not: She doesn't works)*
* *Question:* `Does he handle the database architecture?` *(Not: Does he handles)*

---

### Key Applications
* **Routines & Habits:** *I check my emails first thing every morning.*
* **Unchanging Facts:** *The sun rises in the east.* / *Water boils at 100°C.*
* **State Verbs:** Verbs of mind/emotion (*know, believe, understand, love, rely*) almost exclusively live here. *I **know** exactly what you mean.*

---

### Noticeable Feedback & Common Pitfalls
* ⚠️ **Forgetting the -s:** This is the most common slip-up for intermediate speakers. It's a small error, but highly noticeable. Train your ear to catch: *He **speaks** fluent English,* not *He speak*.
* 💡 **Frequency Adverbs:** Pair this tense with words like *usually, rarely, consistently, down the line, on a regular basis* to elevate your vocabulary.
