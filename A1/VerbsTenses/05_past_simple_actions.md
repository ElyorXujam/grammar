# English Grammar Core: Past Simple (Finished Actions)

### Quick Overview
We deploy the **Past Simple** to talk about actions that started and finished completely in the past. These actions have no direct effect on the present moment, and they are typically anchored to a specific time.

---

### Structural Breakdown

#### 1. Regular Verbs (The simple rule)
Just add **-ed** to the base form. If the verb ends in a consonant + *y*, drop the *y* and add *-ied*.
* *Implement* $ightarrow$ *Implemented*
* *Optimize* $ightarrow$ *Optimized*
* *Rely* $ightarrow$ *Relied*

#### 2. Irregular Verbs (The exceptions)
These do not follow patterns and must be memorized individually.
* *Go* $ightarrow$ ***Went*** | *Have* $ightarrow$ ***Had*** | *Write* $ightarrow$ ***Wrote*** | *See* $ightarrow$ ***Saw***

#### 3. Negatives & Questions (The "Did" Reset Button)
When you use the past auxiliary **did** or **didn't**, the main verb **must return to its base form**. Do not double-conjugate.
* *Positive:* *I **found** the error.*
* *Negative:* *I **didn't find** the error.* *(Not: I didn't found)*
* *Question:* ***Did** you **send** the file?* *(Not: Did you sent)*

---

### Noticeable Feedback & Common Pitfalls
* ⚠️ **The Double Past Tense Blunder:** Watch out for questions and negatives. Once `did` enters the room, the past tense on the main verb leaves. 
    * *Incorrect:* `Where did you went last night?`
    * *Correct:* `Where did you **go** last night?`
